Retinal Disease Detection

Diabetic retinopathy (DR) is a leading cause of blindness worldwide, especially among individuals with diabetes. Early detection is vital for timely treatment and preventing vision loss. However, manual screening of retinal images is time-consuming and prone to human error. This study explores the use of deep learning, specifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), for automated DR detection in retinal fundus images. Using a publicly available dataset of 5,593 labeled images, we train a CNN-based model to classify DR into five severity levels. To enhance model performance, advanced preprocessing techniques such as unsharp masking, contrast enhancement, and circle cropping are applied. The model is evaluated using metrics like precision, accuracy, F1-score, and recall, demonstrating the potential of deep learning for reliable and scalable DR screening. This approach offers a promising solution to reduce the burden on healthcare professionals and improve access to timely care. Additionally, the study discusses challenges such as class imbalance and dataset noise, and suggests ways to improve model generalization and robustness in future work.
